## Key Observations

### What's Working

- **CTR on the Search campaign is strong at 5.04%.** This is above the 3–5% target range for home services, indicating the ad copy and keyword targeting are resonating with searchers.
- **All 5 responsive search ads have "Good" ad strength.** No ads are rated Low or Average. The ad groups are well-structured across distinct services: Crawl Space Mold Treatment, Crawl Space Moisture Control, ERV Installation, Crawl Space Dehumidifier, and Whole House Dehumidifier.
- **Optimization score is 79.3%**, up 3% from prior. The account is in reasonable shape for a new campaign.
- **Relevant search terms appearing.** Queries like "whole home dehumidifier cost" and "triangle crawl space" confirm the targeting is reaching on-topic searchers.

### What Needs Attention

1. **Zero conversions tracked.** This is the most urgent issue. Google's Campaign Diagnostics shows a warning on the Conversions step: "Your website is missing a Google tag." Without the Google tag installed on triangledehumidifiers.com, the account cannot track form submissions or attribute phone calls properly. This makes it impossible to optimize the Maximize Conversions bidding strategy — the algorithm has no conversion signal to learn from.

2. **Average CPC is very high at $27.08.** The typical range for crawl space and dehumidifier keywords in this market is $3–$8. At $27/click, the campaign is spending nearly its entire daily budget on a single click. This is likely caused by the Maximize Conversions bidding strategy bidding aggressively without conversion data to calibrate against. Until the Google tag is installed and conversions are tracked, this will persist.

3. **"Eligible (Limited)" status on the Search campaign.** Google flags two issues:
   - Missing Google tag (conversion tracking)
   - "Targeting fewer searches" — the campaign isn't showing for all relevant queries. Google recommends adding broad match keyword versions.

4. **PMax campaign is paused.** The PMax campaign was generating impressions (mostly irrelevant based on search term data) but no clicks. Before re-enabling, the Google tag needs to be installed and the asset groups should be reviewed.

5. **Several irrelevant PMax search terms appeared before it was paused:**
   - "hvac wake forest nc" — HVAC services, not dehumidifiers
   - "pure air north carolina raleigh" — air purification, not moisture control
   - "newcomb and company" — competitor name
   - "tar heel basement systems" — competitor name
   - "santa fe compact 70 dehumidifier" — retail/portable product
   - "how to insulate crawl space under house" — DIY intent

6. **The Search campaign has a redundant keyword flagged.** Google identified "erv installation near me" as appearing in multiple ad groups with the same destination and bidding strategy.

## Recommended Actions

### Urgent (This Week)

1. **Install the Google Ads tag (gtag.js) on triangledehumidifiers.com.** This is the single most impactful fix. Without it, conversion tracking is broken and the bidding algorithm is flying blind. The tag needs to be added to all pages and configured for phone call conversions and form submissions. This directly explains the $27 CPC — the algorithm has no conversion data to optimize against.

2. **Verify phone call tracking is set up correctly.** Phone calls are 90%+ of home services leads. Confirm that call conversions (Google forwarding number or call tracking) are configured in the Google Ads conversion settings.

3. **Consider switching to Manual CPC temporarily** until conversion tracking is working. Maximize Conversions without conversion data causes erratic, high CPCs. Manual CPC with a $5–$8 max bid would control costs while the tag gets installed and starts collecting data.

### Soon (Next 1–2 Weeks)

4. **Add a business logo to the account.** Google flagged this as a missing asset. Upload the Triangle Dehumidifiers logo to improve ad appearance.

5. **Remove the redundant keyword** ("erv installation near me") from whichever ad group it duplicates in.

6. **Add negative keywords to the Search campaign** to prevent waste if/when PMax is re-enabled:
   - "hvac"
   - "pure air"
   - "newcomb" (competitor)
   - "tar heel" (competitor)
   - "santa fe" (retail product brand)
   - "portable dehumidifier"
   - "DIY"
   - "how to install yourself"
   - "jobs", "hiring", "career"
   - "amazon", "walmart", "home depot"

7. **Do not enable broad match keywords yet.** Google recommends adding broad match versions, but without conversion tracking, broad match will likely increase irrelevant clicks at $27/click. Wait until the tag is installed and at least 2 weeks of conversion data has accumulated.

### Hold (Evaluate After Conversion Tracking Is Live)

8. **Do not turn on AI Max for Search campaigns yet.** Google's top recommendation (+17.7%) is to enable AI Max. This is premature with zero conversion data. Revisit after 30+ conversions are tracked.

9. **Do not re-enable PMax until the Google tag is installed** and the Search campaign is generating measurable conversions. PMax needs conversion signals to optimize properly.

10. **Re-evaluate budget after 2 weeks of tracked conversions.** At $30/day with functional conversion tracking, the campaign should aim for $15–$25 CPA. If CPA is good and budget is consistently maxed, consider increasing to $35–$40/day for the spring/summer peak season.
